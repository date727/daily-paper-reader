Abstract. In recent years, nonconvex minimax problems have attracted significant attention due to their broad applications in machine learning, including generative adversarial networks, robust optimization and adversarial training. Most existing algorithms for nonconvex stochastic minimax problems are developed under the standard Lipschitz smoothness assumption. In this paper, we study stochastic minimax problems under a generalized smoothness condition and propose an algorithm, NSGDA-M, which simultaneously updates the inner variable by stochastic gradient ascent and updates the outer variable by normalized stochastic gradient descent with momentum. In the past decade, many algorithms for nonconvex-strongly concave stochastic minimax problems have been developed under the classical Lipschitz smoothness assumption. Lin et al. [21] propose the stochastic gradient descent with max-oracle (SGDmax), which is a double loop algorithm that alternates between a stochastic gradient descent step on x and an (approx-imate) maximization step on y. Under the standard Lipschitz smoothness assumption on L(В·), the authors show that SGDmax finds an З«-stationary point within ЛңO (Оә3З«вҲ’4)1 stochastic gradient evaluations, where Оә is the condition number for y. Building on this baseline, some double-loop algorithms exploit either the inexact proximal point framework [26, 41] or variance reduction techniques [23, 36] are proposed to achieve better complexity. Moreover, Yang et al. [37] ex-tend SGDmax to an adaptive stepsize variant. Compared to the double-loop algorithms, Lin et al. [21] propose a single loop algorithm, stochastic gradient descent ascent algorithm (SGDA), in which the stepsize О·y is larger than О·x to force y moves faster than x, achieving an O(Оә3З«вҲ’4)stochastic gradient complexity under Lipschitz smoothness condition. SGDA is popular for its simple structure at the cost of complex convergence analysis compared to double loop algo-rithms. Many variants of SGDA are also proposed, including alternating SGDA [2], momentum acceleration SGDA [12, 13], adaptive stepsize SGDA [20, 35] and distributed SGDA [3, 28, 33]. On the other hand, Mahdavinia et al. [24] propose the optimistic gradient descent ascent and extra-gradient methods, which achieve the same stochastic gradient complexity as SGDA. More recently, Yang et al. [38] propose a stochastic smoothed AGDA algorithm for nonconvex-PL minimax problems, which achieves an improved O(Оә2З«вҲ’4) stochastic gradient complexity. However, the classical Lipschitz smoothness assumption is frequently violated in modern machine learning applications, including neural networks [5, 11] and distributionally robust optimization [16], or holds only with a prohibitively large Lipschitz constant L, resulting in overly conservative complexity bounds. To address this challenge, Zhang et al. [40] propose the ( L0, L 1) smoothness condition, which relaxes the global Lipschitz bound by allowing the Hessian norm to grow linearly with the local gradient norm, thereby extending classical Lipschitz smoothness to functions with rapidly growing gradients. The authors show that gradient clipping and normalized gradient methods improve over standard gradient descent in this setting. Later, Zhang et al. [39] extend the definition to accommodate functions that need only be differentiable, and a line of work further extend ( L0, L 1) smoothness to the Оұ-symmetric version [4] and ( в„“0, в„“ 1)-smoothness [18]. Subsequently, numerous algorithms have been proposed for optimization under (L0, L 1) smoothness [5, 9, 14, 19, 22, 27, 32]. Recently, several works have begun to study nonconvexвҖ“strongly concave minimax optimization under generalized smoothness conditions. Xian et al. [34] analyze the deterministic and stochastic variants of GDA and GDAmax under the ( в„“x, в„“ y)-smoothness condition. They show that the generalized GDA and GDAmax achieve an O(Оә2З«вҲ’2) iteration complexity, and their stochastic variants achieve an O(Оә3ОҙвҲ’4З«вҲ’4) sample complexity in high probability for finding an З«-first-order stationary point of the primal function ОҰ( В·) := max yвҲҲY L(В·, y ). Zhu et al. [42] focus on algorithm for finding second-order stationary point under the deterministic setting. They propose an accelerated negative curvature gradient descent ascent algorithm with only gradient calls involved and show that the method achieves a state-of-the-art complexity of O(З«вҲ’1.75 log( n)) under a generalized Hessian smoothness condition. Note that the generalized SGDA and SGDmax in [34] require the batchsize of order Оҳ( З«вҲ’2) for

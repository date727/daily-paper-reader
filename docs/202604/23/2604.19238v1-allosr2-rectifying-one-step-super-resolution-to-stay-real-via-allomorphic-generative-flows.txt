Abstract. Real-world image super-resolution (Real-SR) has been rev-olutionized by leveraging the powerful generative priors of large-scale diffusion and flow-based models. However, fine-tuning these models on limited LR-HR pairs often precipitates “prior collapse” that the model sacrifices its inherent generative richness to overfit specific training degra-dations. This issue is further exacerbated in one-step generation, where the absence of multi-step refinement leads to significant trajectory drift and artifact generation. In this paper, we propose Allo{SR} 2, a novel framework that rectifies one-step SR trajectories via allomorphic gener-ative flows to maintain high-fidelity generative realism. Specifically, we utilize Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) Guided Trajectory Initialization to establish a physically grounded starting state by aligning the degrada-tion level of LR latent features with the optimal anchoring timestep of the pre-trained flow. To ensure a stable, curvature-free path for one-step inference, we propose Flow-Anchored Trajectory Consistency (FATC), which enforces velocity-level supervision across intermediate states. Fur-thermore, we develop Allomorphic Trajectory Matching (ATM), a self-adversarial alignment strategy that minimizes the distributional discrep-ancy between the SR flow and the generative flow in a unified vector field. Extensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world bench-marks demonstrate that Allo{SR} 2 achieves state-of-the-art performance in one-step Real-SR, offering a superior balance between restoration fi-delity and generative realism while maintaining extreme efficiency.

While traditional SR methods [9, 10] achieved remarkable fidelity under predefined synthetic degradation ( e.g ., bicubic down-sampling), the over-smoothing outcomes inevitably fall short in practical sce-narios. Early Real-SR methods leveraged generative adversarial networks (GANs) [12] to recover high-frequency details by pushing the reconstructed outputs to-ward the natural image manifold. However, GAN-based methods [22, 40, 49, 59] are notoriously prone to mode collapse and frequently introduce synthetic ar-tifacts. Recently, diffusion models (DMs) [14, 30, 31, 35, 36] and flow matching (FM) paradigms [3, 4, 25–27] have revolutionized image generation, demonstrat-ing unprecedented capabilities in synthesizing realistic visuals by progressively transforming Gaussian noise into high-quality images. Driven by this success, an emerging trend in Real-SR seeks to adapt these DM- and FM-based generative models as powerful generative priors [24, 39, 47, 53, 57]. By encapsulating rich, universal knowledge of natural image statistics, they facilitate the reconstruc-tion of realistic HR structures. Nevertheless, their inherent iterative sampling nature typically requires tens to hundreds of function evaluations to synthesize a single image, leading to prohibitive latency for practical deployment. To break this efficiency bottleneck, numerous attempts [11, 37, 42, 46, 50, 56, 58,60] have been made to compress the extensive sampling trajectory into a few steps or even one single step. For instance, [58, 60] proposed replacing Gaussian noise with noise-perturbed LR images as the initial state of the diffusion process, thereby significantly reducing the inference steps. SinSR [42] further reformu-lated the inference process of ResShift [58] as an ordinary differential equation (ODE) and directly distilled it into one step. Some other works [50, 56] intro-duced consistency models to constrain the continuous generative trajectories, thereby simplifying the cumbersome iterative sampling into an efficient one-step SR. Beyond these approaches, another active line of research explores score dis-tillation [11, 37, 46, 56], which minimizes the Kullback–Leibler (KL) divergence between the reconstructed outputs and the natural data distribution. Despite these advancements, seamlessly transferring a pre-trained generative prior to a one-step SR task remains a formidable challenge. In the regime of one-step inference, this misalignment—compounded by the lack of velocity-field constraints—leads to a severe trajectory deviation .Consequently, the model suffers from “prior collapse”, where it fails to unleash its inherent generative capacity, resulting in either over-smoothing outputs or hallucinatory artifacts, as visually evidenced in the right column of Fig. 1. While the generative flow provides an optimal, distortion-free path, the SR flow serves as a localized, condition-constrained counterpart. By analyzing the Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR), we demonstrate that the latent features of the LR image are fun-damentally most similar to an intermediate state on the generative trajectory. Consequently, this state naturally serves as the ideal initialization for the local SR process. Instead of standard conditioned fine-tuning, we construct an asym-metric dual-path framework that simultaneously constructs a global generative flow (from noise to HR) and a local SR flow (from the SNR-aligned LR to HR). ## 2 Related Work 

2.1 Real-World Image Super-Resolution 

Image super-resolution (SR) [9,10,20,23,47,59] aims to reconstruct high-resolution (HR) images from degraded low-resolution (LR) observations. Traditional SR methods [9, 10] typically rely on simple synthetic degradations ( e.g ., bicubic downsampling) to synthesize LR-HR training pairs, which limits their effective-ness when applied to complex and unknown degradations present in real world. To bridge this domain gap, generative adversarial networks (GANs) [12,16,64] were introduced to perform real-world SR (Real-SR) with more complex degrada-tions. Pioneering works like SRGAN [20] demonstrated that combining adversar-ial objectives with perceptual losses yields significantly better visual quality than relying solely on pixel-wise constraints. Building upon this, subsequent research shifted focus toward explicit degradation modeling. For instance, BSRGAN [59] incorporates a random shuffle of diverse degradation operators to simulate real-world conditions, providing a robust training baseline. Real-ESRGAN [40] fur-ther extends this paradigm via a high-order degradation modeling process, im-proving the robustness of the data synthesis pipeline and boosting Real-SR per-formance. However, the inherent instability of adversarial training and the lim-ited capacity of discriminators to assess diverse natural image distributions pose significant challenges. Consequently, GAN-based Real-SR methods frequently suffer from mode collapse and are highly prone to generating unnatural visual artifacts. Although subsequent method [22, 41, 49, 63] effectively mitigate these artifacts, they still struggle to synthesize highly natural and photorealistic de-tails, ultimately bounding their generative upper limit. With the success of diffusion models (DMs), recent studies extensively har-ness generative priors for tackle Real-SR task. To balance structural fidelity with perceptual quality, StableSR [39] and DiffBIR [24] adapt pre-trained Stable Diffusion model [32] via specialized feature wrapping or two-stage conditioning frameworks to recover high-frequency details. To further unleash this generative capacity, SeeSR [47] and PASD [53] introduce semantic guidance and pixel-aware cross-attention, enabling the diffusion process to better capture fine-grained local structures and degradation-insensitive features. SUPIR [57] achieves a generative and fidelity capability by scaling up the model and training corpus. Meanwhile, ResShift [58] constructs an efficient diffusion model by shifting the residual be-tween HR and LR. However, these DM-based methods are fundamentally bot-tlenecked by the iterative nature of the reverse denoising process, resulting in an inference time that lags far behind that of GAN-based methods. 

2.2 Acceleration of Diffusion Models and Flow Matching 

Diffusion Models (DMs) [14,30,31,35,36] have achieved remarkable success across various generative tasks by utilizing a parameterized Markov chain to bridge the natural image manifold with the standard Gaussian distribution. Building upon this continuous-time framework, Flow Matching (FM) paradigm [3,4,25] learns a Allo{SR} 2 5

time-dependent velocity field to match the probability flow of a diffusion process. Recent variants, such as Rectified Flow [26, 27], further adjust the reference interpolation path to improve both training stability and convergence. Despite their unprecedented capabilities, the inherent iterative sampling na-ture necessitates tens to hundreds of function evaluations, resulting in severe inference latency. Consequently, accelerating the sampling process has become an important consideration from both theoretical and practical perspectives. Early efforts, such as Progressive Distillation [29, 33] iteratively reduces the in-ference steps of student models through a multi-stage distillation. Neverthe-less, the compounding approximation errors accumulated across these sequen-tial distillation stages significantly undermine the overall performance of the final student model. Meanwhile, Consistency Models [18, 28, 34, 51] achieve effi-cient one-step generation by enforcing a self-consistency property, constraining any intermediate state along the trajectory to map back to the same origin. Recent score distillation approaches, such as Distribution Matching Distillation (DMD) [43, 54, 55], train a one-step generator to match the target distribution by minimizing the Kullback–Leibler (KL) divergence, eliminating the need to strictly follow the teacher’s exact sampling trajectory. Building upon this distri-bution matching paradigm, a recent advancement [6] introduces a novel dual-flow matching mechanism that circumvents the need for auxiliary networks. This ar-chitectural simplification intrinsically eliminates excessive GPU memory cost, allowing for more flexible and scalable training on large models. Driven by these advancements, an emerging line of research seeks to adapt these highly efficient frameworks to the SR task. SinSR [42] reformulates the dif-fusion trajectory into a deterministic sampling process, distilling the inference process into a single step. OSEDiff [46] leverages Variational Score Distillation (VSD) [43, 55] to extract one-step generative capacity from pre-trained multi-step priors. To mitigate the artifacts and instability caused by VSD during early training stages, TSD-SR [11] introduces Target Score Distillation (TSD) for more robust convergence. PiSA-SR [37] seamlessly integrate decoupled LoRA modules, empowering one-step Real-SR with zero-shot, adjustable trade-offs between per-ceptual quality and structural fidelity.

Allomorphic Trajectory Matching While the FATC in Eq. 10 regularizes the velocity field, fine-tuning pre-trained generative model on limited LR-HR data pairs often precipitates “prior collapse”. In this regime, the model sacrifices its generative richness to overfit the specific degradations of the training pairs. To circumvent this, we propose Allomorphic Trajectory Matching (ATM). Rather than adapting the generative prior to the SR task, ATM aligns the SR trajectory with the intrinsic generative flow. Inspired by the self-adversarial framework of TwinFlow [6], we construct two allomorphic, parameter-shared trajectories within a unified vector field: a gen-erative flow and an SR flow. By sharing weights across both tasks, the model is forced to learn a unified latent representation where SR transitions are perceived as a subset of the broader generative probability flow. ## 4 Experiments 

4.1 Experimental Settings Training Datasets Following [46, 47], we train our model on LSDIR [21] and the first 10K face images from FFHQ [16], totaling 95K images. The degradation pipeline of Real-ESRGAN [40] is used to synthesize LR-HR training pairs. 

Testing Datasets We evaluate our method on a synthetic dataset DIV2K-Val [1] and three real-world datasets, including RealSR [5], DRealSR [45] and RealLQ250 [2]. We crop 512 × 512 patches from DIV2K-Val and apply the Real-ESRGAN degradation pipeline to downsample them to 128 × 128 . For RealSR and DRealSR, which provide paired GT images, we apply center-cropping to the LR images to a resolution of 128 ×128 . Since RealLR200 lacks corresponding GT images, we perform no cropping and maintain the original 256 × 256 resolution. All experiments are conducted with the scaling factor of ×4.

Compared Methods We compare Allo{SR} 2 with other DM- and FM-based models, including multi-step models: StableSR [39], SeeSR [47], ResShift [58]; and one-step models: SinSR [42], OSEDiff [46], TSD-SR [11], and CTMSR [56]. 

Evaluation Metrics To comprehensively evaluate the performance of different methods, we combine full-reference and no-reference metrics. PSNR and SSIM [44] are full-reference fidelity measures, while LPIPS [62] and DISTS [8] are full-reference perceptual quality metrics. FID [13] assesses the distributional distance between the original and reconstructed images. For blind quality assessment of no-reference images, we combine NIQE [61], MUSIQ [17], MANIQA [52], and CLIPIQA [38] to evaluate the naturalness and structural integrity of the images. 

Implementation Details We utilize the pre-trained weights of FLUX.1-dev [19] as our foundation generative model. To facilitate efficient parameter adap-tation, LoRA [15] is integrated into both the VAE encoder and the Diffusion Transformer (DiT) components with a rank of r = 64 . The framework is opti-mized using the AdamW optimizer [7] with a learning rate of 5 × 10 −5 and a global batch size of 16. The training process is conducted on 8 NVIDIA A100 GPUs for a total of 10K iterations.

The Impact of LFATC The primary objective of LFATC is to linearize the super-resolution trajectory, ensuring that a single-step Euler integration can ac-curately approximate the underlying probability flow.

## 5 Conclusion 

In this paper, we have presented Allo{SR} 2, a novel flow-matching framework that redefines one-step Real-world Super-Resolution as a specialized navigation within a generative manifold. By identifying the allomorphic nature of image generation and restoration, we move beyond the conventional paradigm of naive latent substitution, which often suffers from prior collapse and trajectory de-viation. Technically, our SNR-guided initialization and Flow-Anchored Trajec-tory Consistency (FATC) effectively bridge the domain gap between degraded observations and generative priors, ensuring a curvature-free restoration path. Furthermore, the Allomorphic Trajectory Matching (ATM) strategy facilitates a self-adversarial alignment that successfully preserves the inherent richness of pre-trained models. Extensive experiments validate that Allo{SR} 2 achieves a superior balance between pixel-wise fidelity and generative realism in a single NFE. Ultimately, this work demonstrates that the rectification of restoration trajectories via structural correlation with generative flows is a promising path toward efficient, high-fidelity vision foundations in the wild.

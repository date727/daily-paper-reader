## 摘要
我们研究了Mendelson、Paouris和Vershynin提出的对抗性伪造检测模型。在此模型中，真实样本服从\pmb{X}\sim N(0,\pmb{I}_n)，而伪造样本则由\pmb{X}+r\pmb{t}({\pmb{X}})生成，其中对手首先观察\pmb{X}，然后从给定的集合\mathscr{T}\subset\mathbb{R}^n中选择一个允许的扰动\pmb{t}({\pmb{X}})。核心量是检测半径r(\mathscr{T})，它形式化了伪造样本与真实样本可靠区分的过渡尺度。Mendelson、Paouris和Vershynin引入了聚焦宽度\widetilde{w}(\mathscr{T})作为该半径的几何参数，并猜想对于每个原点对称集\mathscr{T}，它在通用常数范围内表征r(\mathscr{T})。在本说明中，我们反驳了这一猜想在广泛离散集类中的有效性。更具体地说，我们考虑任何位于超立方体与奇整数网格之间的原点对称集\mathscr{T}_n：\begin{equation*} \{-1,1\}^n\subset\mathscr{T}_n\subset ( 2\mathbb{Z}+1)^n. \end{equation*} 对于每个这样的\mathscr{T}_n，我们证明\frac{\widetilde{w}(\mathscr{T}_n)}{r(\mathscr{T}_n) }\gtrsim \sqrt{\log n}。因此，在高斯模型中，聚焦宽度可能比检测半径高估一个\sqrt{\log n}因子，因此一般情况下无法表征其检测半径。我们进一步表明这种对数尺度并非本质性的：在对应的乘积拉普拉斯数据非高斯模型中，聚焦宽度基准甚至可能至少超过检测半径一个n^{1/4}阶多项式因子。

## Abstract
We study the adversarial fake detection model introduced by Mendelson, Paouris and Vershynin. In this model, a genuine sample is $\pmb{X}\sim N(0,\pmb{I}_n)$, while a fake sample is produced as $\pmb{X}+r\pmb{t}({\pmb{X}})$, where the adversary first observes $\pmb{X}$ and then chooses an admissible perturbation $\pmb{t}({\pmb{X}})$ from a prescribed set $\mathscr{T}\subset\mathbb{R}^n$. The central quantity is the detectability radius $r(\mathscr{T})$, which formalizes the transition scale at which fake samples become reliably distinguishable from genuine ones. Mendelson, Paouris and Vershynin introduced the focused width $\widetilde{w}(\mathscr{T})$ as a geometric parameter for this radius and conjectured that, for every origin-symmetric set $\mathscr{T}$, it characterizes $r(\mathscr{T})$ up to universal constants.   In this note, we disprove this conjecture for a broad class of discrete sets. More precisely, we consider any origin-symmetric set $\mathscr{T}_n$ lying between the hypercube and the odd integer grid: \begin{equation*} \{-1,1\}^n\subset\mathscr{T}_n\subset ( 2\mathbb{Z}+1)^n. \end{equation*} For every such $\mathscr{T}_n$, we prove that $\frac{\widetilde{w}(\mathscr{T}_n)}{r(\mathscr{T}_n) }\gtrsim \sqrt{\log n}$. Thus, in the Gaussian model, the focused width can overestimate the detectability radius by a $\sqrt{\log n}$ factor and therefore does not characterize it in general. We further show that this logarithmic scale is not intrinsic: in the corresponding non-Gaussian model with product Laplace data, the focused width benchmark can even exceed the detectability radius by at least a polynomial factor of order $n^{1/4}$.
